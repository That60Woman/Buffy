# License Philosophy

Buffy is licensed under the GNU Affero General Public License v3 (AGPL-3.0).

That decision wasn't made accidentally.

---

## Why AGPL?

Buffy exists because calendars have become fragmented, proprietary, and increasingly disconnected from reality.

Our goal is not to build another scheduling platform.

Our goal is to establish a shared, open scheduling engine that anyone can inspect, improve, and trust.

If Buffy succeeds, it should become infrastructure—not a product controlled by a single company.

The AGPL helps protect that future.

Unlike permissive licenses, the AGPL requires that improvements to the software remain available to the community, including when the software is modified and operated as a network service. This closes the "SaaS loophole" that exists with GPL-licensed software and ensures that organizations benefiting from Buffy contribute improvements back to everyone.

---

# Freedom, Not Ownership

Buffy is built around a simple belief:

**Reality should not be proprietary.**

Nobody owns:

* weekdays
* business hours
* sunrise
* school schedules
* statutory holidays
* buffer time
* travel time
* family commitments

These are facts about reality.

Buffy simply describes them.

The software should therefore remain just as open as the reality it models.

---

# Community Before Vendors

Every software project eventually faces the same temptation:

> Build an open community...
>
> then close the source once people depend on it.

Buffy rejects that path.

We would rather have thousands of contributors than thousands of customers locked into proprietary infrastructure.

If a company builds on Buffy, wonderful.

If they improve Buffy, even better.

If they offer hosted services, consulting, integrations, enterprise support, or custom implementations, that's exactly how healthy open-source ecosystems grow.

But improvements to the scheduling engine itself should remain part of the commons.

---

# Sustainability Matters

Open source is not incompatible with sustainable businesses.

Organizations can build commercial products, support contracts, managed hosting, consulting, integrations, and enterprise services around Buffy while the core scheduling engine remains open.

The AGPL encourages reciprocity:

If you improve the engine that everyone relies on, everyone should benefit from those improvements.

# Trust Through Transparency

Scheduling software quietly makes thousands of decisions.

What counts as "busy"?

When should buffers be added?

Which calendars have priority?

What happens when rules conflict?

Those decisions should never be hidden behind proprietary code.

Users deserve to understand why Buffy reached a particular conclusion.

Transparency is a feature.
