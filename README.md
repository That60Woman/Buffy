# Buffy

**Reality-based scheduling for real life.**

> Buffy doesn't own your calendar. Buffy keeps it faithful to reality.

Buffy is an open-source scheduling engine that maintains availability calendars from user-defined rules instead of requiring users to manually manage every change.

Rather than asking:

> "What am I doing on Tuesday?"

Buffy asks:

> "Given everything that is true about my life, what time is actually available?"

## Why Buffy?

Most calendar systems are event databases. They remember what you entered.

Life doesn't work that way.

Availability changes because of school pickups, commute times, recurring commitments, medical appointments, shared calendars, holidays, maintenance windows, travel, and hundreds of other realities.

Buffy continuously derives availability from those realities.

## Core Principles

- Reality is the source of truth.
- Rules are more valuable than manual edits.
- Calendars should be reproducible.
- Availability should be explainable.
- Users own their data.

## Status

Buffy is currently in active design and early development.
