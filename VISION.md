# Buffy Vision

> **Buffy doesn't own your calendar. Buffy keeps it faithful to reality.**

---

# Why Buffy Exists

Calendars are excellent at remembering what we've already decided.

They are terrible at representing reality.

Modern scheduling tools ask people to become manual synchronization engines. Every change in life requires someone to remember every calendar, every recurring event, every booking page, every travel buffer, every exception, every holiday, every family obligation, every volunteer role, and every personal commitment.

The result isn't simply inconvenience.

It's drift.

Over time, the calendar stops representing reality and starts representing a collection of forgotten assumptions.

People miss appointments.
Businesses overbook.
Families double-book.
Volunteers disappear.
Partners assume availability that no longer exists.
Entire scheduling systems slowly become fiction.

Most calendar software responds by adding more interfaces.

More buttons.
More integrations.
More notifications.
More AI assistants.

But none of those solve the underlying problem.

The problem isn't remembering events.

The problem is maintaining a faithful model of availability as reality changes.

Buffy exists to solve that problem.

---

# The Core Idea

Availability is not entered.

Availability is derived.

People rarely decide every individual free hour on their calendar.

Instead, availability emerges from rules.

Examples:

* I don't book appointments after 4:00 PM.
* Tuesdays are for art.
* Never schedule over my spouse's work commute.
* Leave one hour between coaching sessions.
* Don't drive on Sundays.
* If school is closed, childcare changes everything.
* If I'm travelling, block preparation time.
* If a meeting is online, no travel buffer is required.

Humans already think this way.

Calendars generally do not.

Buffy does.

---

# Reality First

Buffy treats calendars as observations, not truth.

Google Calendar isn't reality.

Outlook isn't reality.

Apple Calendar isn't reality.

CalDAV isn't reality.

Those are merely observations about scheduled events.

Reality is produced by applying constraints, relationships, priorities, and rules.

Buffy continuously rebuilds availability from those facts.

Whenever something changes...

...a new meeting...
...a cancelled event...
...a partner's schedule...
...a holiday...
...a travel requirement...

Buffy recalculates what is actually possible.

The calendar becomes a projection of reality rather than a fragile artifact that must be maintained by hand.

---

# Buffy Is Not Another Calendar

Buffy deliberately avoids becoming yet another place to manage events.

Existing calendar applications are already good at:

* viewing events
* editing events
* accepting invitations
* reminders
* notifications
* synchronization

Replacing those systems adds little value.

Instead, Buffy sits above them.

It observes.

Reasons.

Calculates.

Publishes.

Users continue using the calendars they already know.

Buffy simply ensures those calendars remain faithful to reality.

# Principles

## Reality over preference

Reality wins.

Rules may express preferences, but conflicts are always resolved according to reality.

If two commitments overlap, they overlap.

No interface can wish that away.

---

## Rules over repetition

If something happens repeatedly, it should become a rule.

Manual maintenance is technical debt.

---

## Deterministic before intelligent

Buffy should always be explainable.

Every blocked hour should answer:

> "Why?"

Every available hour should answer:

> "Why?"

Reasoning should be inspectable.

Users should never have to trust a black box.

---

## Explicit over implicit

Rules should be visible.

Priorities should be visible.

Conflicts should be visible.

Assumptions should never be hidden.

---

## Compose, don't customize

Instead of endless option screens, Buffy composes simple rules.

Small rules combine into surprisingly sophisticated behaviour.

---

## Local reasoning

Every rule should describe one piece of reality.

Large behaviour emerges naturally from many small constraints.

---

## Portable by design

Buffy belongs to the user.

Rules should remain human-readable.

Configurations should be version controlled.

Nothing should require a proprietary cloud.

---

## Open first

Buffy is designed as open-source software.

Its greatest value comes from transparency.

People should understand how availability is calculated.

Organizations should be able to audit it.

Developers should be able to extend it.

---

# What Buffy Is

Buffy is a reality-based scheduling engine.

It consumes observations from one or more calendars.

It applies user-defined rules.

It produces an accurate model of availability.

Everything else is implementation.

---

# What Buffy Is Not

Buffy is not:

* another calendar application
* another booking website
* another AI assistant
* another notification system
* another task manager

It performs one job exceptionally well:

Maintaining the truth about availability.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Eventually, Buffy should become the scheduling equivalent of Git.

Not because everyone uses the same interface.

Because everyone trusts the engine underneath.

Applications will come and go.

Calendars will evolve.

Booking platforms will change.

But reality-based scheduling should remain stable.

Developers should be able to build on top of Buffy instead of rebuilding scheduling logic from scratch.

Organizations should be able to express their operational constraints as code.

Families should be able to describe their lives as understandable rules.

Individuals should regain confidence that "free" on the calendar actually means free.
